# Minimal Axioms and Derived Structure of HCSN

**Hierarchical Closure Spacetime Networks (HCSN)** proposes that spacetime geometry
emerges from discrete relational dynamics governed by closure, hierarchy,
and defect-mediated regulation.

This document presents a **minimal axiom system**, followed by **derived theorems**
that are directly supported by simulation results.

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

The goal of this document is to:

- State the *smallest possible* set of axioms needed to generate spacetime-like behavior
- Separate **assumptions** from **observations**
- Show how observed laws follow logically from the axioms
- Provide a stable foundation for future work (particles, fields, gravity)

No continuum assumptions are made.

## 2. Fundamental Axioms

### Axiom 1 — Discrete Relational Substrate

The fundamental structure is a finite (but unbounded) **hypergraph** \( H \),
composed of:
- vertices (events)
- hyperedges (relations)
- causal ordering relations

There is **no background metric, geometry, or manifold**.

> Geometry is not assumed — it must emerge.

---

### Axiom 2 — Local Rewrite Dynamics

The hypergraph evolves through **local rewrite rules** acting on finite subgraphs.

Each rewrite:
- is local
- is finite
- preserves causal consistency
- may be probabilistically accepted or rejected

This defines the fundamental notion of dynamics.

---

### Axiom 3 — Causal Consistency

No rewrite may introduce a closed causal loop.

Formally:
\[
\forall v \in H,\quad v \not\prec v
\]

This ensures a well-defined emergent arrow of time.

---

### Axiom 4 — Finite Information Propagation

Each rewrite step modifies only a bounded amount of relational information.

Consequences:
- No instantaneous global influence
- Finite correlation length
- Computable evolution

---

### Axiom 5 — Closure Tension Principle

Rewrite dynamics favor configurations that increase **relational closure**
while suppressing excessive redundancy.

This is not a maximization rule, but a **tension** between:
- closure formation
- hub concentration suppression

This principle regulates emergent geometry.

---

### Axiom 6 — Defect Permissibility

Rewrite rules allow **localized violations** of closure balance,
producing discrete **topological defects** characterized by a charge \( \Delta Q \).

Defects:
- are localized in time and structure
- occur discretely
- modify hierarchical closure

## 3. Emergent Observables

From the evolving hypergraph, the following observables are defined:

- \( \langle k \rangle \): average coordination
- \( \Omega \): hierarchical closure (multi-scale relational stability)
- \( \Phi \): interaction concentration
- \( \Psi \): closure density
- \( L \): maximal causal chain length

These quantities are **not axioms** — they are measurements.

## 4. Derived Theorems (Supported by Simulation)

### Theorem 1 — Emergence of Nonzero Hierarchical Closure

**Claim:**  
A stable, nonzero value of \( \Omega \) must emerge dynamically.

**Sketch:**  
Local rewrites increase relational connectivity (Axiom 2), while closure tension
(Axiom 5) prevents runaway redundancy. The system converges to a stable hierarchical regime.

---

### Theorem 2 — Hierarchical Closure Is an Attractor

**Claim:**  
Perturbations in \( \Omega \) decay over time.

**Sketch:**  
Excess closure imbalance triggers defect events (Axiom 6), which restore structural balance.
This produces attractor-like behavior.

---

### Theorem 3 — Defects Occur as Discrete Events

**Claim:**  
Topological defects are discrete in time and magnitude.

**Sketch:**  
Rewrite rules are discrete (Axiom 2), and defect permissibility is local (Axiom 6).
Thus defects occur as isolated events.

---

### Theorem 4 — Defect Charge Produces Finite \( \Delta \Omega \)

**Claim:**  
Each defect induces a finite jump in hierarchical closure.

**Sketch:**  
Defects alter local hierarchical structure, and \( \Omega \) aggregates hierarchy globally.
Finite local change implies finite global response.

---

### Theorem 5 — Defect Rate Tracks Geometric Fluctuation

**Claim:**  
The defect rate increases with variance in \( \Omega \).

**Sketch:**  
High variance indicates unstable geometry. Defects act as regulatory events,
leading to a positive correlation between variance and defect frequency.

---

### Theorem 6 — Finite Autocorrelation of Geometry

**Claim:**  
Hierarchical closure has finite temporal autocorrelation.

**Sketch:**  
Finite information propagation (Axiom 4) and continuous rewrites decorrelate structure,
preventing long-range memory.

---

### Theorem 7 — Renormalization Stability of Geometry

**Claim:**  
\( \Omega \) is approximately invariant under coarse-graining.

**Sketch:**  
Coarse-graining removes microscopic noise while preserving hierarchical relations.
This indicates emergent geometric scale invariance.

---

### Theorem 8 — Coordination Growth Does Not Destabilize Geometry

**Claim:**  
Increasing \( \langle k \rangle \) does not destroy hierarchical closure.

**Sketch:**  
Coordination measures density, not hierarchy. Closure tension (Axiom 5)
suppresses hub dominance, preserving structure.
